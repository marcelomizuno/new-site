01 — COLLECTION

Information we collect

We collect only what we need to deliver the service, run the business, and stay in touch. Nothing more.

When you visit this website, fill in a contact form, book a diagnosis call, or become a client, we collect the following categories of information:

  • Identity & contact details — name, email, phone number, business name, role, and country. You provide these directly through forms, email, or scheduled calls.
  • Business context — industry, team size, current revenue infrastructure (CRM, calendar, ad accounts), and the specific problem you’re trying to solve.
  • Technical data — IP address, browser type, device class, operating system, referring URL, and pages viewed. We collect this through standard server logs and analytics.
  • Cookies & tracking pixels — see section 03 for the full list of cookies and how to opt out.
  • Communication history — emails, WhatsApp messages, call recordings (only when we tell you first), and notes from working sessions.
Heads up: if you submit a form on behalf of someone else (e.g. a colleague), you confirm you have their consent to share their personal data with us.
02 — USE

How we use information

Five lawful purposes. Nothing is sold, and nothing is used for advertising outside the context of our own services.

We process personal data only when we have a valid legal basis to do so. In practice that means:

  • To deliver the service you asked for — build, install, and operate the revenue infrastructure we agreed on in the proposal.
  • To communicate with you — reply to enquiries, send project updates, share invoices, and confirm meetings.
  • To improve our service — aggregate, de-identified analytics about how the website is used, which pages convert, and where the funnel leaks.
  • To meet legal and tax obligations — keep the records we are required to keep (invoices, contracts) for the period required by local law.
  • To send relevant updates — only with your consent, and only the kind of update you actually signed up for (a newsletter, a case study, a release note).

We do not sell, rent, or trade personal data with third parties for their own marketing purposes. We do not run retargeting campaigns against people who have asked us not to.

03 — COOKIES

Cookies & similar technologies

A short, honest list. If you don’t want non-essential cookies, you can reject them or clear them at any time.

Cookies are small text files placed by your browser when you visit a website. We use them in three buckets:

  • Strictly necessary cookies — keep you logged in, remember your language preference, and protect the site from abuse. These cannot be turned off because the site breaks without them.
  • Analytics cookies — anonymous, aggregated traffic and behaviour data (page views, scroll depth, session duration) used to improve the site. We use privacy-respecting analytics and never identify individual visitors.
  • Marketing cookies — only set after explicit consent. Used to measure the effectiveness of our own campaigns. We do not run third-party ad retargeting without consent.

You can change your preferences at any time via your browser settings (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge all support granular cookie controls) or by clearing the site’s cookies in your browser’s storage panel. The site will continue to work, but preferences like language may reset to the default.

04 — THIRD PARTIES

Third parties we share data with

We share data with a short list of vetted processors — and only the data each one needs to do its job.

The full list of sub-processors is below. Each operates under a written data-processing agreement and complies with applicable privacy law (LGPD, GDPR, CCPA, as relevant).

  • Hosting & infrastructure — a tier-1 cloud provider hosting this website, our project dashboards, and client data backups. Data is stored in the region we specify per client.
  • Email & calendar — transactional email service and calendar booking tool used to send confirmations, reminders, and meeting invites.
  • WhatsApp Business API provider — only for clients who opt in. We use a Meta-approved partner. Conversations are scoped per client business number.
  • Analytics — privacy-respecting analytics provider that does not require consent banners in most jurisdictions.
  • Payment processor — when you pay an invoice, payment data goes directly to our PCI-compliant processor. We never see or store your card number.
  • Professional advisors — our lawyers and accountants, when strictly necessary to comply with legal obligations or defend a legal claim.

If a sub-processor changes, we update this page within 30 days and notify active clients by email. If a client objects, they can terminate the affected service without penalty.

05 — CONTACT

Your rights & how to reach us

You own your data. We’ll honour any reasonable request within the timeframes required by applicable law.

Depending on where you live, you may have the right to:

  • Ask what personal data we hold about you (right of access).
  • Correct inaccurate or incomplete data (right to rectification).
  • Request deletion of your data, subject to legal retention obligations (right to erasure).
  • Restrict or object to certain processing activities.
  • Receive your data in a portable, machine-readable format (right to portability).
  • Withdraw consent at any time, where processing is based on consent.
  • Lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority.

To exercise any of these rights, email privacy@brand.example from the address we hold on file. We respond within 15 business days. For identity verification we may ask for a piece of information only you would know (e.g. the date of your last invoice).

Our Data Protection Officer (DPO) is the primary point of contact for all privacy questions, complaints, and regulator requests. Postal address available on request.

Still have a privacy question?

We’d rather answer it directly than hide behind legal copy. Reach the DPO at privacy@brand.example and you’ll hear back from a human within one business day.
